About

Hi! I’m Professor Rafael de Mello, a Brazilian Software Engineering researcher and practitioner. I have over 20 years of industry experience, mainly in the financial and banking sectors. A skeptical surfer in the sea of empiricism, I’m currently trying to ride the Generative AI wave on a surfboard called evidence. In recent years, my work has focused on bridging the gap between Software Engineering research and practice. My research group (Iter) is a pioneer in applying social psychology theories—such as social representations and social identity—to Software Engineering. My publications include award-winning papers in venues such as ICSE, FSE, and ESEM. I also recognize myself as a recovering “Reviewer 2,” striving to provide constructive and empathetic feedback. :)
